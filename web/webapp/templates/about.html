General

This website currently holds the data captured at a monitoring station on the banks of the River Thames near Clifton Hampden, Oxfordshire. See more information at this Position.

A modern monitoring station is a structure placed somewhere outside, and they will have various detectors attached to them. It will typically record things like sound, images, and the weather in its area. And Ultimately, it is there to detect the wildlife and measure the biodiversity.

Wikipedia links

Some of the data displayed on the species pages comes directly from Wikipedia and its associated websites. This is stated on each page when used, but in general, it can be assumed that the descriptions and photos of a species come from those excellent resources.
For example the images on the Robin page are taken from its corresponding Wikimedia page.
Thank you to the people who make their work free so that websites like this one can exist.

Data and the datasets

This website is divided up in to several different types of data. You will most likely be interested in the Species data set, but the other parts also contain useful information on how the species data was collated.

The five different datasets included in the website - Analyses, Detections, Detectors, Positions and Species - are each described below. But in simple terms:

  • A Position (i.e. a monitoring station) has several Detectors (e.g. a camera).
  • Each Detector captures a specific kind of data (e.g. a camera captures a photo).
  • An Analyses scans the data to see if it can find a Species. (There's a bird in the photo).
  • A Detection records when a species was found. (On Monday, there was a Blue Tit at the monitoring station!).

Analyses dataset

This dataset includes processes that have been run to identify a Species. This might be a simple piece of software, an AI trained to identify a species from a sound, or in a special case, a human.

In the case of AI based analyses, these will typically include "confidence estimates", and these show "how sure" a process is that it made the right determination. On this website only confidence higher than 0.5 are included (the confidence scale got from 0.0 to 1.0).

An Analyses is designed to run on a particular Detector.

See a list of analyses here.

Detections dataset

This dataset stores each individual detection of a Species. It records the name of the species, at what time and date is was detected, and what Analyses was used.

See a list of detections here.

Detectors dataset

Detectors are the physical devices used to capture a piece of data. A typical example would be a camera or or a microphone. They are located at a certain Position.The data each collects is run through a certain Analyses.

See a list of detectors here.

Positions dataset

This is the precise location and description of a monitoring station. It has a list of the detectors attached.

Although it is possible for this website to record more than one monitoring station, it is currently only being used for a single one.

See the position here

Species dataset

These are all the species detected. Bats, Birds and Bugs being the main ones. For instance, a Gray Wagtail or a Long-Eared Bat.

See a list of species here.
